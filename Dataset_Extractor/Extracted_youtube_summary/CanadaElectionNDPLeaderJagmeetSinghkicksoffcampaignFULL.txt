The federal election is officially a go.Prime Minister Justin Trudeau made the announcement on Wednesday in Ottawa shortly after a meeting with Governor General Julie Payette at Rideau Hall.“A few moments ago, I met with Her Excellency the Governor General, who granted my request to dissolve Parliament,” he told reporters, speaking in French.“Canadians will go to the polls on Oct. 21.”Under federal elections law, this coming Sunday marked the last possible date for the start of a federal campaign.Those campaigns must be at least 36 days long and cannot exceed 50 days.
