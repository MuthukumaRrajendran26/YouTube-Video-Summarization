The Ontario government said Saturday that it has confirmed 151 new cases of COVID-19 in the province, bringing the total number of cases to 1,144. The provincial death toll remained unchanged at 18.Associate chief medical officer of health Dr. Barbara Yaffe said Saturday that details about exposure were missing in 45 per cent of cases, but said in the confirmed cases where information was available, 16 per cent were the result of community-transmission.Earlier on Saturday, Premier Doug Ford issued an emergency order that could see price gougers of essential items face a fine of $759 or, if summoned and convicted in court, could face a fine of $1,000 and one year in jail.
