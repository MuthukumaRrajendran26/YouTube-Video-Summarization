Canadian Minister of Foreign Affairs, François-Philippe Champagne, Patty Hajdu, Minister of Health and Dr. Theresa Tam, Chief Public Health Officer, provided an update on the Canadians aboard the Grand Princess cruise ship, which is set to dock on Monday in Oakland, Calif., after being quarantined at sea for several days after 21 passengers aboard tested positive for the COVID-19.The Canadians aboard the ship will be transported to CFB Trenton in Ontario, where they will be quarantined for an additional 14 days.Chief Public Health Officer Dr. Theresa Tam said that the Public Health Agency of Canada recommends Canadians should avoid all cruise ship travel, in light of the COVID-19 outbreak and spread.
