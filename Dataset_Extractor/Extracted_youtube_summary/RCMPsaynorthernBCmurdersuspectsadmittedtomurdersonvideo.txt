The three people murdered in northern B.C. by two young men this summer were killed by gunshot wounds, with casings found at both crime scenes matching each other.The information was released by RCMP Friday along with its exhaustive investigative findings into the murders, including the pieces of evidence that linked Bryer Schmegelsky and Kam McLeod to the deaths.The evidence included six videos on a digital camera found with the young men’s bodies in rural Manitoba, where they take responsibility for the three murders and announce plans to kill themselves.
