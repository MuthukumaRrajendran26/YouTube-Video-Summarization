The strike by Ontario's education workers, which would have seen 55,000 clerical workers, custodians and early childhood educators walk the picket line, was averted on Sunday evening after a deal was reached between the union, CUPE, and the government. But Premier Doug Ford's government will now have to turn its attention to negotiations with teachers.Travis Dhanraj reports on what those talks could mean for students after one strike was narrowly averted.
