Britain's lower house of parliament, the House of Commons, will sit on Wednesday after the U.K. Supreme Court ruled Prime Minister Boris Johnson's suspension of parliament for five weeks in the crucial countdown to the country's Brexit deadline was unlawful, Commons Speaker John Bercow said."I have instructed the House authorities to prepare not for the recall - the prorogation was unlawful and is void - to prepare for the resumption of the business of the House of Commons," he told reporters on Tuesday morning.The landmark Supreme Court decision immediately prompted calls for Johnson to quit.Johnson is travelling in New York for the U.N. General Assembly and hasn’t commented yet on the adverse ruling.
