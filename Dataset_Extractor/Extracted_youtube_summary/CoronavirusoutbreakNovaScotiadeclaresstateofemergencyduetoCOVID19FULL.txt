Nova Scotia's Premier Stephen McNeil announced a state of emergency for the province due to COVID-19, closing their borders and giving law enforcement the power to enforce self-isolation and social distancing.Sunday's announcement was made during a briefing by officials including chief medical officer Dr. Robert Strang, on the provincial response to the COVID-19 pandemic.Nova Scotia announced on Saturday that they’ve identified six additional cases of COVID-19 in the province.The total number of cases has now climbed to 21, nine of which are confirmed and 12 of which are presumptive.
