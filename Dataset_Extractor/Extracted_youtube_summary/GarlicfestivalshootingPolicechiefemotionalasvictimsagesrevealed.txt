On Monday Gilroy Police Chief Scot Smithee confirmed three people, including a six-year-old boy, were killed when a gunman opened fire at the city's famous garlic festival Sunday.Gilroy Police Chief Scot Smithee said the gunman was armed with a rifle and sneaked in through a fence that borders a parking lot next to a creek. He appeared to randomly target people when he opened fire just after 5:30 p.m. Sunday, the conclusion of the three-day festival that attracts more than 100,000 people to the city known as the “Garlic Capital of the World.”
