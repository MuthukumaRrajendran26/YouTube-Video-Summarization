Canadian Foreign Affairs Minister Chrystia Freeland on Monday called it a "mistake" for China to label protesters in Hong Kong as "terrorists," adding that governments should listen carefully to the concerns of their people.Freeland said Canada is "looking closely" at the situation in Hong Kong and advised Canadians to exercise a "high degree of caution" when visiting the city.Canada's foreign affairs minister also spoke about allegation's from U.S. President Donald Trump that China is manipulating its currency, but declined to say directly whether Canada agrees with the Trump administration on this issue, pointing instead to the International Monetary Fund. Freeland said there could be "a lot of explanations" for any change in the Chinese currency.
